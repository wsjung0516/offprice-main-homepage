Summary

This program is a website for clothing closeout sales. It enables sellers to sell their inventory in bulk quantities. Wholesale or closeout sales businesses can use this website to select and purchase items. The program allows users to store selected items in a separate cart and initiate transactions with the sellers by verifying the seller's information provided with the product. The program focuses on the following considerations:

  • Fast search: Ensuring users can quickly find their desired items among a large number of products.
  • Easy product registration for sellers: Designing a user-friendly interface for product registration without the need for extensive instructions.
  • Responsive mode: Applying responsive design to enable easy product registration and browsing on any hardware device. It particularly allows direct photo uploads from mobile devices.

    Reasons for development:

    1. Started developing the website due to the recommendation of an acquaintance, as there are many sellers with a large inventory in the LA jobber market. The goal was to create a website for these sellers to sell closeout products.
    2. Initially, thorough market research was necessary, but there was a lack of information on wholesale and closeout sales. Therefore, the design relied on the experience of some small-scale traders.
    3. The focus of development was to create a system that is easy for sellers to use, rather than prioritizing the buyer's perspective. However, there were limitations in obtaining sufficient information for this aspect.
    4. Therefore, the development was initially based on strategic considerations.
    5. To commercialize the website, advertising and additional development plans would be necessary. However, due to the lack of resources to compete with existing competitors large company such as "Faire" and "Fashion-Go", the development was temporarily halted.

    Development period:

  1. 3.5 months (2023-03-01 to 2023-06-15)

    2. Technologies applied in development:

    1. Angular: Developed using the latest version, incorporating both standalone and SSR (Server Side Rendering) capabilities.
    2. Nestjs: Chosen as the back-end technology due to its similarities with Angular in terms of structure, making it easy to apply for those familiar with Angular. It is also highly scalable and suitable for large-scale projects.
    3. Prisma: Applied as an ORM (Object Relational Model) technology, which is easier to implement compared to TypeORM and provides fast performance.
    4. Docker: Containerized the front-end and back-end programs.
    5. Kubernetes: Utilized for simplified deployment of the containerized front-end and back-end programs, enabling easy application of CI/CD, continuous monitoring, and self-healing capabilities for stable operations. Ingress and reverse proxy were implemented for Nestjs and Angular.
    6. MySQL: Google Cloud SQL was used for stable data backup, monitoring, and automatic scaling.
    7. Firebase: Implemented for user authentication purposes.
